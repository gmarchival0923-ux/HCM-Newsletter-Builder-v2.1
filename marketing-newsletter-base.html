Lead Story

The M&A Wave in HR Tech Isn't Slowing Down. Your Marketing Strategy Needs to Catch Up

- Maren Hogan is the CEO and Founder of Red Branch Media

Here's something I don't think we're talking about enough in HR Tech: the volume of private equity and M&A activity in this space isn't a blip. It's the new operating environment. And most marketing leaders... most agencies... most go-to-market playbooks? They were built for a world that doesn't exist anymore.

I've been in B2B marketing for 25 years (20 of those in HR Tech specifically), and Red Branch Media has worked with over 200 B2B tech companies during that time. I'm not saying that to flex. I'm saying it because the pattern I'm about to describe only becomes visible after you've watched it happen across dozens of companies, and it took me a while to spot it myself.

PE-backed HR Tech is a structurally different operating environment. And the sooner marketers treat it that way, the sooner they stop getting blindsided by it.

Hot Marketing Takes

SHRM State of AI in HR 2026 Report reveals that 87% of CHROs are forecasting deep integration of AI within their HR processes this year. Interestingly, the report highlights a massive wave of AI "FOMO" (fear of missing out); roughly one-third of HR professionals whose companies already use AI mistakenly feel they are lagging behind their competitors.

As AI-driven transformation pushes HR into a deep partnership with IT, 64% of IT leaders now predict a complete merger of the two functions within five years. Gartner also notes that evolving an HR operating model to accommodate this human-machine era yields a 29% boost in productivity.

For HR vendors and sales teams, this means the era of selling exclusively to HR is over; marketing and sales professionals must adopt a "dual-buyer" approach, tailoring collateral to simultaneously prove strategic workforce value to the CHRO and seamless, secure data governance to the CIO.
